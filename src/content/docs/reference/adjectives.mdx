## Adjective Degrees

<Tabs>
    <TabItem label="comparative">
        Uses 3rd declension (consonant) endings

        masc / fem: stem + ior (nom)

        neut: stem + ius (nom/acc)
        

        <Details summary="example" open="true">
            <AdjectiveDeclensions json={json} word="melior"/>
        </Details>
    </TabItem>
    <TabItem label="superlative">
        stem + issim + 1st / 2nd declension endings

        stem (ending in "r" or "er") + errim + 1st / 2nd declension endings

        stem (ending in "l" or "il") + illim + 1st / 2nd declension endings

        <Details summary="example" open="true">
            <AdjectiveDeclensions json={json} word="optimus"/>
        </Details>
    </TabItem>
</Tabs>


## Adverb Degrees

<Tabs>
    <TabItem label="positive">
        1st / 2nd stem + e

        3rd stem + iter
        
        <Details summary="example" open="true">
            1st / 2nd stem: pulchrior (masc nom) -> pulchre
            
            3rd stem: celer (masc nom) -> celeriter
        </Details>
    </TabItem>
    <TabItem label="comparative">
        stem + ius (same as singular neuter of comparative adjective)
        
        <Details summary="example" open="true">
            1st / 2nd stem: pulchrior (masc nom) -> pulchrius
        </Details>
    </TabItem>
    <TabItem label="superlative">
        stem + issim + e

        stem (ending in "r" or "er") + errim + e

        stem (ending in "l" or "il") + illim + e

        <Details summary="example" open="true">
            tūtus (masc nom) -> tutissime

            pulchrior (masc nom) -> pulcherrime

            facilis (masc nom) -> facillime
        </Details>
    </TabItem>
</Tabs>
